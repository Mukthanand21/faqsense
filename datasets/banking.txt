Q: What is a bank
A: A bank is a regulated financial institution that accepts deposits from the public, safeguards money, and provides financial services such as loans, payments, and investment products.

Q: What is a savings account
A: A savings account is a deposit account designed to encourage saving by offering interest on balances while allowing limited withdrawals.

Q: What is a current account
A: A current account is primarily used by businesses for frequent transactions and usually does not earn interest.

Q: What is a fixed deposit
A: A fixed deposit is an investment where funds are deposited for a fixed tenure at a predetermined interest rate.

Q: What is a recurring deposit
A: A recurring deposit allows customers to invest a fixed amount regularly and earn interest over time.

Q: What is net banking
A: Net banking enables customers to perform banking transactions online through secure bank websites.

Q: What is mobile banking
A: Mobile banking allows customers to access banking services through mobile applications anytime and anywhere.

Q: What is an ATM
A: An Automated Teller Machine allows customers to withdraw cash and perform basic banking services without visiting a branch.

Q: What is KYC
A: Know Your Customer is a regulatory process used to verify customer identity to prevent fraud and money laundering.

Q: Why is KYC important
A: KYC ensures the authenticity of customers and helps banks comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

Q: What documents are required for KYC
A: Common KYC documents include proof of identity, proof of address, and recent photographs.

Q: What is a debit card
A: A debit card allows direct access to funds in a bank account for payments and withdrawals.

Q: What is a credit card
A: A credit card allows customers to borrow money up to a credit limit and repay it later with interest if unpaid.

Q: What is an overdraft facility
A: An overdraft facility permits account holders to withdraw more money than their account balance up to an approved limit.

Q: What is a bank statement
A: A bank statement provides a detailed record of transactions over a specific period.

Q: How can account balance be checked
A: Account balance can be checked using ATMs, mobile banking, net banking, or by visiting a branch.

Q: What is interest rate
A: Interest rate is the percentage charged on borrowed funds or earned on deposits.

Q: What is a home loan
A: A home loan is a long-term loan provided to purchase, construct, or renovate residential property.

Q: What is a personal loan
A: A personal loan is an unsecured loan provided for personal financial needs without collateral.

Q: What is a car loan
A: A car loan is a secured loan offered for purchasing vehicles.

Q: What is EMI
A: Equated Monthly Installment is a fixed payment made monthly to repay a loan.

Q: What is loan tenure
A: Loan tenure is the duration over which the loan is repaid.

Q: What is foreclosure of a loan
A: Foreclosure is the complete repayment of a loan before its scheduled end date.

Q: What is prepayment
A: Prepayment refers to partial repayment of the loan principal before the due date.

Q: What is credit score
A: A credit score is a numerical measure of an individual's creditworthiness.

Q: How can credit score be improved
A: Credit score improves through timely repayments, low credit utilization, and responsible borrowing.

Q: What is bank reconciliation
A: Bank reconciliation is the process of matching bank records with company or personal records.

Q: What is nominee in banking
A: A nominee is a person entitled to receive account proceeds after the account holder’s death.

Q: What is cheque
A: A cheque is a written instrument directing a bank to pay a specified amount.

Q: What is demand draft
A: A demand draft is a prepaid banking instrument guaranteeing payment.

Q: What is NEFT
A: National Electronic Funds Transfer allows electronic transfer of funds between banks.

Q: What is RTGS
A: Real Time Gross Settlement enables immediate high-value fund transfers.

Q: What is IMPS
A: Immediate Payment Service allows instant fund transfer 24x7.

Q: What is banking fraud
A: Banking fraud involves unauthorized or deceptive financial activities.

Q: What is account freezing
A: Account freezing restricts transactions due to compliance or legal reasons.

Q: What is minimum balance
A: Minimum balance is the required amount to maintain in an account.

Q: What is service charge
A: Service charge is a fee collected for banking services.

Q: What is passbook
A: A passbook records account transactions manually or electronically.

Q: What is interest compounding
A: Compounding calculates interest on both principal and accumulated interest.

Q: What is inflation impact on savings
A: Inflation reduces the real value of money over time.

Q: What is bank merger
A: A bank merger combines two or more banks into a single entity.

Q: What is core banking
A: Core banking enables customers to access services from any branch.

Q: What is digital banking
A: Digital banking provides financial services through electronic channels.

Q: What is an insurance policy
A: An insurance policy is a legal contract providing financial protection against specific risks.

Q: What is insurance premium
A: Premium is the amount paid periodically to keep insurance coverage active.

Q: What is policyholder
A: A policyholder is the individual who owns the insurance policy.

Q: What is insurer
A: An insurer is the company that provides insurance coverage.

Q: What is sum assured
A: Sum assured is the guaranteed amount payable under an insurance policy.

Q: What is life insurance
A: Life insurance provides financial security to beneficiaries after death.

Q: What is term insurance
A: Term insurance offers life coverage for a fixed term at affordable cost.

Q: What is health insurance
A: Health insurance covers medical and hospitalization expenses.

Q: What is motor insurance
A: Motor insurance protects vehicles against accidents theft and liabilities.

Q: What is claim in insurance
A: A claim is a request made to receive policy benefits.

Q: What is deductible
A: Deductible is the amount the insured pays before claim settlement.

Q: What is policy renewal
A: Policy renewal extends coverage after policy expiry.

Q: What is grace period
A: Grace period allows delayed premium payment without policy lapse.

Q: What is policy lapse
A: Policy lapse occurs when premiums are unpaid beyond grace period.

Q: What is cashless treatment
A: Cashless treatment allows hospitals to settle bills directly with insurers.

Q: What is network hospital
A: Network hospitals are partnered with insurers for cashless services.

Q: What is no claim bonus
A: No claim bonus rewards policyholders for not making claims.

Q: What is rider in insurance
A: A rider is an additional coverage option added to a policy.

Q: What is reinsurance
A: Reinsurance is insurance purchased by insurers to reduce risk.

Q: What is underwriting
A: Underwriting evaluates risk before issuing insurance.

Q: What is insurance fraud
A: Insurance fraud involves false claims or misrepresentation.

Q: What is nominee in insurance
A: A nominee receives policy benefits after the policyholder’s death.

Q: What is surrender value
A: Surrender value is the payout received on early policy termination.

Q: What is maturity benefit
A: Maturity benefit is the payout received at policy completion.

Q: What is insurance coverage
A: Insurance coverage defines the risks protected by the policy.

Q: What is claim settlement ratio
A: Claim settlement ratio measures insurer claim settlement performance.

Q: What is insurance exclusion
A: Exclusions are situations not covered under an insurance policy.

Q: What is finance
A: Finance involves managing money including investing saving borrowing and budgeting.

Q: What is personal finance
A: Personal finance focuses on managing individual financial activities.

Q: What is budgeting
A: Budgeting is planning income and expenses.

Q: What is an investment
A: Investment allocates money to assets expecting future returns.

Q: What are stocks
A: Stocks represent ownership shares in a company.

Q: What are bonds
A: Bonds are debt instruments offering fixed returns.

Q: What is mutual fund
A: Mutual fund pools money from investors to invest in diversified assets.

Q: What is SIP
A: SIP allows regular investments in mutual funds.

Q: What is compound interest
A: Compound interest earns interest on principal and accumulated interest.

Q: What is simple interest
A: Simple interest is calculated only on principal.

Q: What is inflation
A: Inflation increases prices reducing purchasing power.

Q: What is diversification
A: Diversification reduces risk by spreading investments.

Q: What is liquidity
A: Liquidity measures ease of converting assets into cash.

Q: What is net worth
A: Net worth equals assets minus liabilities.

Q: What is financial planning
A: Financial planning helps achieve short and long-term goals.

Q: What is retirement planning
A: Retirement planning ensures income after retirement.

Q: What is tax planning
A: Tax planning minimizes tax liability legally.

Q: What is emergency fund
A: Emergency fund covers unexpected expenses.

Q: What is risk in finance
A: Risk is uncertainty of returns or losses.

Q: What is return on investment
A: ROI measures investment profitability.

Q: What is capital gain
A: Capital gain is profit from asset sale.

Q: What is financial literacy
A: Financial literacy improves money management skills.
